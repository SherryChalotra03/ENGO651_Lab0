About Me

I’m Sherry Chalotra, a passionate researcher, educator, and problem-solver committed to advancing the frontiers of Artificial Intelligence, Geomatics Engineering, and optimization. With a robust academic background and hands-on experience in both teaching and research, I thrive at the crossroads of cutting-edge technology and practical applications.

With a strong work ethic, adaptability, and a genuine love for learning, I bring enthusiasm and dedication to every challenge I take on. Whether collaborating with a team or working independently, I excel at quickly mastering new skills and delivering meaningful results.

My journey has been fueled by curiosity and a drive to make impactful contributions. From optimizing software development costs using Bee Colony Optimization to navigating complex geospatial challenges with PostGIS, my work bridges theory and practice. Currently, as a PhD student, I’m exploring the exciting realm of Vision-Language Navigation (VLN), delving into how AI can better interpret spatial and visual data for seamless navigation in dynamic environments.

When I’m not immersed in research or keeping up with the latest tech trends, I cherish moments spent recharging with my husband and friends. Whether it’s discovering cozy cafes, soaking in the beauty of nature, or planning exciting adventures, these experiences fuel my creativity and keep me grounded.
